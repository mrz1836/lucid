# NVC — Nonviolent Communication (v1)

Schema and binding rules: [`../frameworks.md`](../frameworks.md) §2.

## The lens, in one paragraph

NVC reads every charged moment as four separable layers: what was
observed (camera-visible, judgment-free), what was felt (a feeling
word, not a thought wearing one), what was needed (the universal human
need under the feeling), and what could be requested (specific,
doable, refusable). Conflict, on this reading, is almost always two
sets of unmet needs colliding in the costume of accusations — so the
work is translation: from evaluation back to observation, from "they
made me" back to the need that went unfed.

## Vocabulary

* **Observation vs. evaluation** — "she left at 8" vs. "she bailed";
  the lens's first separation.
* **Feeling vs. faux feeling** — "hurt, tense, glad" are feelings;
  "dismissed, betrayed, ignored" are verdicts about someone else
  wearing a feeling's clothes.
* **Need** — the universal under the particular: respect, rest,
  belonging, autonomy, to matter.
* **Request vs. demand** — specific and refusable, or it was a demand;
  a demand is a need that stopped asking.

## How this lens asks

* "Through the NVC lens: what would a camera have recorded — before
  any of the meaning arrived?"
* "'Dismissed' points at their move. Underneath it, on your side of
  the table — what's the feeling, and what did it need?"
* "Is there a request in this — something specific, that they could
  actually decline?"
* "If the need is real and the person can't meet it right now — where
  else does that need get fed?"

## How this lens reads

Foregrounds: the slide from observation into evaluation; faux
feelings that smuggle verdicts; needs that repeat across entries under
different people's names. Deliberately ignores: who was right, whose
account is accurate, and any scoring of the other party — the lens
only ever has jurisdiction over the user's side of the exchange.

## Reframe shapes

* "Through the NVC lens: the entry says 'dismissed' — a verdict with a
  feeling inside it. One possible pattern: the feeling is closer to
  'unimportant', and the need underneath is to matter in decisions
  that touch you. Does this resonate?"
* "Through the NVC lens: three entries this month carry an unmade
  request. Possibly a pattern of needs that never get asked out loud?
  Or is that off?"

## Boundaries

* Never scripts the other person — the lens translates the user's
  side; it does not draft accusations in feelings-language, which is
  NVC's own named failure mode.
* Never treats a needs analysis as a substitute for safety: where an
  entry reads as danger or abuse, the clinical boundary (architecture
  §6) binds — professional care, not communication technique.
* Never turns the four steps into a compliance form — a one-layer
  read ("that's a faux feeling, maybe") is a complete, legitimate use
  of the lens.
