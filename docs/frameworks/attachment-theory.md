# Attachment Theory (v1)

Schema and binding rules: [`../frameworks.md`](../frameworks.md) §2.
**This file is the licensing exemplar** (frameworks.md §6): the lens
cannot speak without its pattern names, so it licenses exactly two
blocklist patterns — noun-phrase forms only, hypothesis-framed only,
under its own label only. The certainty forms (*"you're an anxious
attacher"*, *"you have avoidant tendencies"* as verdicts) remain
unlicensable under every lens, including this one.

## The lens, in one paragraph

Attachment theory reads closeness-under-stress as patterned: when
connection feels threatened, people reach (protest, pursue,
re-check), or retreat (deactivate, go self-sufficient, go quiet), or
do both in sequence — patterns learned early, running fastest exactly
when it matters most. On this reading, a fight about the dishes is
rarely about dishes; it is about whether reaching gets answered. The
lens's use is noticing *the move under stress*, not filing people
into types.

## Vocabulary

* **Bid / reach** — the small move toward connection that risks going
  unanswered.
* **Protest behavior** — the reach in armor: the pointed silence, the
  re-text, the picked fight that means "notice me."
* **Deactivation** — the retreat move: distance, busyness,
  self-sufficiency narratives ("I'm fine alone anyway").
* **Anxious / avoidant / secure patterns** — directions of the
  under-stress move; **patterns in moments, not verdicts on people.**
* **Earned security** — the documented finding that patterns move
  with practice and safe relationships; the reason this lens is worth
  running at all.

## How this lens asks

* "Through the attachment lens: when it started to feel far away,
  what was your first move — toward, away, or both in a row?"
* "Was there a reach in this that went out in armor — a protest that
  meant 'notice me'?"
* "What would the answered version of that bid have looked like?"
* "Under all of it — what did closeness need, and what felt like it
  would happen if you asked plainly?"

## How this lens reads

Foregrounds: the move-under-stress repeating across different people;
bids that go out disguised; deactivation narratives arriving right
after vulnerable entries. Deliberately ignores: typing the other
person (their inner life is not in evidence), childhood etiology (the
record may show it someday; the lens does not presume it), and any
use of pattern names as identity.

## Reframe shapes

* "Through the attachment lens: one possible pattern — an anxious
  pattern shows up in these three entries: the re-text, the checking,
  the relief when she answered. A pattern in moments, not a verdict
  on you. Does this resonate?"
* "Through the attachment lens: 'I'm fine alone anyway' lands the
  same evening you wrote about wanting to be asked to stay — possibly
  a deactivating move right after a reach felt risky. Does that fit,
  or is it off?"

## Boundaries

* **Patterns, never types** — binding. The licensed vocabulary names
  behavior in evidence ("an anxious pattern in these entries"), never
  identity ("you're anxious"). The unlicensable certainty forms stay
  banned; this file's licenses do not and cannot touch them.
* **Never types third parties.** The lens has jurisdiction over the
  user's own moves only; partners and friends are never assigned
  patterns.
* **Not assessment, not diagnosis** — no instrument is being
  administered, and the clinical boundary (architecture §6) binds:
  where entries read as relational trauma territory, the move is
  professional care with longitudinal context, not more labeling.
* Earned security is the frame: every pattern surfaced is surfaced as
  movable, because that is what the research actually says.

## Sources
