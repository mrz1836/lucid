# IFS — Internal Family Systems (v1)

Schema and binding rules: [`../frameworks.md`](../frameworks.md) §2.
Read the Boundaries section first; for a therapy-derived lens it is
the load-bearing part.

## The lens, in one paragraph

IFS reads inner conflict as a family of parts rather than a single
divided self: protective parts that manage life or douse pain, young
parts that carry old burdens, and underneath them a calm center
(Self) that can turn toward any of them with curiosity instead of
being flooded by them. On this reading, "I don't know why I did that"
usually means a part acted while another part watched — so the work is
never to defeat a part, only to get curious about what it protects.

## Vocabulary

* **Part** — a distinct inner stance with its own feelings and job
  ("a part of me wanted to leave; another part kept smiling").
* **Protector** — a part whose job is preventing pain: the planner,
  the critic, the pleaser.
* **Firefighter** — a protector that acts *after* pain hits, fast and
  expensive: the doomscroll, the drink, the slammed door.
* **Exile** — a young part carrying an old burden, usually what the
  protectors are protecting.
* **Self** — the calm, curious center that can host the rest;
  "self-energy" is its felt presence.
* **Blending** — when a part's voice temporarily *is* your voice.

## How this lens asks

* "Through the IFS lens: it sounds like part of you wanted X while
  another part did Y. Can you say more about each, separately?"
* "The critic showed up hard in this entry. If it has a job — what is
  it protecting you from, do you think?"
* "Was there a moment you were blended — where the part's voice was
  the only voice? What did it need right then?"
* "Can you find any curiosity toward that part, even a little? What
  does it say back?"

## How this lens reads

Foregrounds: same-part signatures across entries (the same protector
under different situations); the protector/exile pairing (harsh inner
voice following a vulnerable moment); moments of self-energy worth
naming because they are evidence the calm center exists. Deliberately
ignores: which part is "right", and any inventory-building ambition —
the map stays exactly as detailed as the user's own words make it.

## Reframe shapes

* "Through the IFS lens: one possible pattern — the harsh voice shows
  up right after you've been openly hopeful about something. If it's
  a protector, it may be guarding against the drop. Does this
  resonate?"
* "Through the IFS lens: 'I kept smiling but I was gone' reads like
  two parts in the same minute — a pleaser out front, and someone who
  left. Worth meeting them separately, or is that off?"

## Boundaries

* **This lens is IFS-informed vocabulary, not IFS therapy** — binding.
  No unburdening protocols, no guided exile work, no trauma
  processing: that is wound-level work and belongs with a trained
  human (architecture §6). The lens maps; it never treats.
* Never asserts a part into existence — parts language is offered
  only over material where the user's own words carry the
  multiplicity ("part of me…", "I don't know why I…").
* Never names an exile's burden for the user; where an entry gestures
  at old pain, the lens's whole move is the professional-care
  boundary plus the record's longitudinal context.
* Never pathologizes: parts are never disorders, and firefighters are
  never moral failures — every part has a job.
