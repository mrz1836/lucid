# The Four Agreements (v1)

Schema and binding rules: [`../frameworks.md`](../frameworks.md) §2.
This is a **book-scoped lens** (frameworks.md §8): one book's four
practices, not the whole Toltec tradition — the lineage line says
exactly how far it reaches.

## The lens, in one paragraph

The Four Agreements holds that most suffering runs on invisible
agreements absorbed early — about what we are, what we owe, what
others' behavior means — and offers four to replace them: **be
impeccable with your word** (the word creates; used against yourself
or others, it poisons), **don't take anything personally** (what
others do is a projection of their own reality, not a verdict on
yours), **don't make assumptions** (ask, instead of mind-reading),
and **always do your best** (which changes hour to hour, and is the
end of self-judgment, not a whip). The lens reads an entry by asking
which agreement was under strain — and whether the harshest sentence
in the entry was one the user aimed at themselves.

## Vocabulary

* **The word** — everything said, including self-talk and the
  retelling of others; impeccability means not using it against
  anyone, yourself first.
* **Taking it personally** — importing someone else's weather as a
  verdict about you.
* **The assumption** — the story built where a question could have
  stood; mind-reading presented to yourself as fact.
* **Your best (today's)** — indexed to the day's actual capacity,
  not to an ideal day's; doing it ends the case against yourself.
* **Domestication** — Ruiz's name for the absorbed agreements the
  four exist to replace; used lightly, as history rather than
  diagnosis.

## How this lens asks

* "Through the Four Agreements lens: which agreement was under strain
  here — the word, the personal, the assumption, or the best?"
* "Is there a story in this entry doing the work of a fact — an
  assumption that could simply be asked?"
* "If their behavior was about their day and not about you, what's
  left of this entry?"
* "What did *today's* best honestly look like — on the day you
  actually had?"

## How this lens reads

Foregrounds: assumptions wearing the costume of facts ("they think
I'm useless"); the word turned inward (the entry's harshest sentence
aimed at the self); personalization of others' weather; "best"
measured against an imaginary day rather than the declared one — this
lens dovetails exactly with the capacity signal and modes
([`../engine.md`](../engine.md) §2): a capacity-2 best is a complete
best, and the mode system already says so in arithmetic. Deliberately
ignores: whether the other person was in fact wrong (not this lens's
jurisdiction), and any use of "agreement" language as a scorecard.

## Reframe shapes

* "Through the Four Agreements lens: 'they think I'm useless' reads
  like an assumption doing a fact's job — one possible pattern:
  mind-reading shows up exactly where asking feels risky. Does this
  resonate?"
* "Through the Four Agreements lens: the sharpest sentence in this
  entry is aimed at you — the word used against yourself. Does that
  fit, or is it off?"
* "Through the Four Agreements lens: this reads like a capacity-2 day
  measured against a capacity-5 standard. 'Always do your best' means
  *that* day's best. Does this land?"

## Boundaries

* **"Don't take it personally" never dismisses a legitimate
  grievance** — and never, ever applies to harm, threat, or abuse;
  where an entry reads as danger, the clinical boundary (architecture
  §6) binds and this lens stands down entirely.
* **"Always do your best" is never a productivity whip.** The lens
  reads best through declared capacity and mode — it exists to end
  self-judgment, and any use of it that generates obligation is a
  misuse by definition (the sanctuary rule holds: words are never
  scored, not even against agreements).
* The agreements are never a scorecard — no counting kept/broken
  agreements, no streaks, no per-agreement tallies. Which-was-strained
  is a question, not a metric.
* Not doctrine: Ruiz's Toltec framing is his; the lens serves a user
  who chose it and never argues cosmology.
