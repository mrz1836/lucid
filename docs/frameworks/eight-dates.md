# Eight Dates (v1)

Schema and binding rules: [`../frameworks.md`](../frameworks.md) §2.
This is a **book-scoped lens** (frameworks.md §8): one practice from
one book, not a whole tradition — the lineage line says exactly how
far it reaches.

## The lens, in one paragraph

Eight Dates holds that a lasting relationship is not a state but a
set of conversations that never finish — and that couples quietly
stop having them on purpose, until they only happen by ambush. The
eight territories: **trust & commitment, addressing conflict, sex &
intimacy, work & money, family, fun & adventure, growth &
spirituality, dreams.** The lens reads relationship entries by asking
which conversation this moment actually belongs to, and when that
conversation was last had deliberately — over a table, as a date —
rather than mid-fight, mid-logistics, or not at all.

## Vocabulary

* **The eight territories** — the conversations listed above; most
  charged moments live in one of them wearing another's costume
  (money dressed as chores, trust dressed as a calendar dispute).
* **Perpetual vs. solvable problems** — most relationship conflict
  (the Gottmans' research says roughly two-thirds) is perpetual:
  managed through understanding, never solved. Treating a perpetual
  problem as solvable is its own failure mode.
* **Open-ended question** — a question that cannot be answered with
  yes/no and is not an accusation in costume.
* **The date** — the deliberate container: a conversation held on
  purpose, prepared for, protected from logistics.
* **Turning toward** — meeting a small reach with attention; the
  micro-act the dates exist to practice at scale.

## How this lens asks

* "Through the Eight Dates lens: which of the eight conversations is
  this entry actually about — and which costume is it wearing?"
* "When did the two of you last have that conversation on purpose,
  rather than by ambush?"
* "Does this read as a solvable problem or a perpetual one? They're
  handled differently — one gets a fix, the other gets understood."
* "What's the open-ended question you'd actually want them to answer
  this week?"

## How this lens reads

Foregrounds: the same territory recurring across entries in different
costumes; territories that never appear at all (an unvisited
conversation is data); conflict entries treated as solvable that keep
returning (perpetual-problem signature). Deliberately ignores:
scoring the partner, adjudicating who was right, and any reading of
the partner's inner state — their side of the conversation is not in
evidence.

## Reframe shapes

* "Through the Eight Dates lens: three entries this month sit in the
  work-and-money territory, and each arrived as logistics friction.
  One possible pattern: the money conversation is happening only by
  ambush. Does this resonate?"
* "Through the Eight Dates lens: this fight looks perpetual — same
  shape in March, same shape now. Perpetual problems get understood,
  not solved. Worth a date in the conflict territory, or is that
  off?"

## Boundaries

* **The aim is always the conversation with them, never a better
  archive about them.** A lens that made journaling about the
  relationship more satisfying than talking in it would be doing harm
  with good vocabulary; when the record starts substituting for the
  table, that is the pattern worth surfacing.
* Never assigns the partner anything — no homework, no diagnosis, no
  pattern names. Jurisdiction is the user's side of the table only.
* **Not couples therapy.** The Gottman method is clinical work; this
  lens is one book's conversation practice. Where entries read as
  contempt, fear, or harm, the clinical boundary (architecture §6)
  binds: professional care, with longitudinal context.
* If the user wants the dates themselves as a commitment with teeth
  (a monthly date as a practice), that is an Engine act through a
  Gate — the lens interprets; it never schedules.
